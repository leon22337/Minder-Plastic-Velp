Why This Matters

The Plastic Bag Problem

Plastic bags seem harmless — until you follow their journey after the bin.

🌊

They End Up in Nature

Millions of bags end up in rivers, parks, and oceans every year. They break into microplastics that enter the food chain — including the food on your table.

⏳

They Last 500+ Years

A single plastic bag takes up to 500 years to decompose. Used for 10 minutes, it outlives every person alive today — many times over.

🐢

Wildlife Suffers

Sea turtles mistake bags for jellyfish. Birds feed them to chicks. Over one million marine animals die from plastic pollution every single year.

🏘️

Velp Is Affected Too

Plastic litter is found along the IJssel riverbanks, in Velp's parks, and on neighbourhood streets. Our local environment deserves better.

The Solution

Reusable Is the Future

Small changes in habit make a massive collective difference.

👜

Bring Your Own Bag

Keep a tote in your coat pocket or backpack. You'll never need a plastic bag again.

♻️

Choose Paper Over Plastic

When you forget your bag, pick paper. It biodegrades in weeks, not centuries.

🗣️

Ask the Supermarket

Supermarkets respond to customer feedback. A polite email from many people creates real change.

👥

Spread the Word

Share this page with friends and family in Velp. Community pressure works.

💡

Did you know? One reusable cotton bag replaces up to 700 plastic bags over its lifetime. That's 700 bags that never enter the environment.
